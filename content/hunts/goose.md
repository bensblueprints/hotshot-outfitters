## Why Michigan's Thumb Produces Goose Hunting

Geese need two things: food and water, in close proximity. Huron County delivers both in abundance.

The agricultural landscape — corn, soybeans, wheat, and winter wheat stubble — provides exactly the caloric resources that geese need to fuel migration and build fat reserves through fall. Large fields with low obstruction on the edges are prime goose feeding terrain, and Huron County has miles of it. The Lake Huron shoreline and the inland lakes, ponds, and drainages that dot the landscape provide the roosting water geese need within easy commuting distance of the food.

Michigan's Canada goose population is divided between resident geese — birds that nest and live here year-round — and migratory geese that move through from Ontario, Quebec, and further north. The resident population is substantial and provides early-season hunting opportunity. As fall progresses and northern birds push south, hunting can intensify dramatically, particularly after the first hard freeze that locks up northern water and pushes birds ahead of it.

## Michigan Canada Goose Season Dates

Goose seasons in Michigan are set cooperatively between the U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service and the MI DNR under the Migratory Bird Treaty Act framework. Seasons are established annually and dates can change year to year. General planning reference:

- **Early Canada Goose Season (Resident Birds)**: Typically early to mid-September, before the regular waterfowl season. This season specifically targets the resident Canada goose population. Bag limits and zones apply.
- **Regular Canada Goose Season**: Typically begins in mid-October and runs through portions of December, concurrent with regular waterfowl season. Zone-specific dates apply in Michigan — verify your specific zone at the MI DNR.
- **Conservation Order / Light Goose Season**: Michigan may offer extended seasons for specific goose populations under conservation order frameworks. Verify current availability with MI DNR.

As always, regulations change annually. Bag limits, season dates, and zone boundaries are set each fall. Verify current rules at the [MI DNR website](https://www.michigan.gov/dnr) and the U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service migratory bird regulations before your hunt.

## What to Expect on a Guided Goose Hunt

A goose hunt with Hotshot Outfitters is set up for success from the day before your hunt. Here's how it works:

**Scouting**: Your guide scouts field feeding activity in the days leading up to your hunt. We're watching where geese are entering fields, which direction they approach from, and what time they're moving. That scouting informs exactly where we set up and how we configure the decoys.

**Field Setup**: We'll be in the field before first light. Layout blinds or natural ground blinds are set up to be as flush with the ground surface as possible. The decoy spread — full bodies, shells, windsocks, or a combination — is positioned to present a natural-looking loafing and feeding flock with open landing zones in front of the blinds where birds will be in range when they commit.

**Calling**: Canada goose calling is one of the most important skills in waterfowl hunting. The cluck, moan, double-cluck, and laydown call are the basics; building a calling sequence that sounds like a relaxed feeding flock is an art. Electronic callers are legal for Canada geese in many contexts — your guide will manage calling or can walk you through the sequences if you want to call.

**The Kill Zone**: When birds commit and drop into the spread, the guide will call the shot at the right moment — when birds are in range, slowing, and presenting clean shooting angles. Discipline in the blind (staying still, faces down) until the call is given is what turns a working flock into dead birds in the decoys.

## Field Hunting vs. Water Hunting

Canada goose hunting in Huron County offers both field and water setups, and the right choice depends on season, conditions, and where birds are concentrated on a given week.

**Field Hunting**: Layout blind hunting in cut grain fields is the most common approach for Canada geese on agricultural ground. Full-body decoy spreads in a feeding configuration, with the blinds buried in the spread, produce close-range shooting on committed birds. This is classic Midwest goose hunting and it's the backbone of our guided operations.

**Water and Shoreline Hunting**: Lake Huron points, inland lakes, and pond setups offer alternative opportunities, particularly for birds that are roosting on specific water bodies and can be intercepted early morning before they leave for the fields. Water hunting requires different equipment (boat or wader setup) and a different decoy presentation, but it can produce excellent action when birds are using the water.

Both are available through Hotshot Outfitters. We'll recommend the approach that best fits current bird behavior when you book.

## Resident Geese vs. Migrant Geese: Different Hunting Challenges

Michigan's resident Canada goose population is large, well-established, and present year-round. These birds grow up around agriculture and around human activity — they're not naive. Early season resident geese have seen decoys before, have heard bad calling, and will flare from a sloppy setup faster than migrant birds. Hunting them requires disciplined concealment, realistic decoy spreads, and quality calling.

Migratory Canada geese from northern Ontario and Quebec that arrive in late October and November are, in many cases, birds that have had limited exposure to hunting pressure. They tend to be more responsive to calling and more willing to commit to a decoy spread that's not perfect. When a flight of northern birds drops into Michigan ahead of a cold front, the hunting quality changes noticeably.

Our guides know the difference and adjust their approach accordingly. Early season means slower, more patient setups with premium attention to concealment. Mid-season migration means being flexible about location and capitalizing on bird movement windows.

## Licensing and What You Need

Every goose hunter in Michigan must hold:

- **Valid Michigan Hunting License** with a **Michigan Waterfowl License/Duck Stamp**
- **Federal Duck Stamp** — required for all waterfowl hunters 16 and older
- HIP registration (Harvest Information Program) — free registration required for all migratory bird hunters in Michigan; register when purchasing your license
- Non-residents must purchase appropriate non-resident Michigan licenses

Waterfowl licensing in Michigan involves multiple components. Do not arrive at the field without all required credentials. Confirm the current licensing stack with the MI DNR when you purchase your license.

## Gear Notes for Canada Goose Hunting

Canada geese are big, tough birds. You need shotgun loads that deliver adequate energy at 35–50 yard ranges. For steel shot, BB or BBB in a 12-gauge 3" or 3.5" shell is the standard. If you're shooting a 3.5" magnum, steel BB is an excellent choice for clean kills on body shots. Premium non-toxic loads (HEAVYWEIGHT, TSS) allow smaller shot sizes at higher energy levels — TSS in #9 or #8 will kill geese cleanly at ranges that would be marginal with steel, but the cost is substantially higher.

Modified or improved modified choke is correct for steel shot in most 12-gauge shotguns. Do not shoot BB or larger steel through full choke.

For layout blind hunting, wear camouflage that matches the field background. If we're hunting a harvested corn field with brown stubble, wear brown/tan patterns — not green timber camo. Your guide will advise on the field conditions before your hunt.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What is the bag limit for Canada geese in Michigan?**

Bag limits for Canada geese are set annually and zone-specifically under the Migratory Bird Treaty framework. Limits vary by zone and by goose population designation (Temperate Breeding vs. southern James Bay population, etc.). Verify current limits at the MI DNR and USFWS websites before each season — they change year to year.

**Q: When is the best time of year to hunt Canada geese in Michigan's Thumb?**

The early September resident goose season can provide high-volume shooting on local birds before they've been pressured. Mid-to-late October through November, when northern migrants arrive, often produces the most dynamic hunting as flights of new birds work well into decoys. Your guide will advise based on current year conditions.

**Q: Do I need special equipment for layout blind goose hunting?**

You need layout blinds (we provide these), camouflage that matches the field, and a facemask that covers everything except your eyes. Geese look directly down into the spread when they commit, and they will see an uncovered face at 30 yards. Full concealment is not optional in field goose hunting.

**Q: Can I bring my own hunting dog on a goose hunt?**

Trained retrievers are welcome. Discuss at booking and we'll plan the setup accordingly. An untrained dog in a field goose setup will cause problems — confirm your dog's capability before bringing them.

**Q: Is goose hunting available in the early September season?**

Yes. The early Canada goose season targeting resident birds typically opens in early September in Michigan. This is a popular hunt for clients who want goose hunting before regular waterfowl season opens. Contact us about early season availability.

**Q: What if birds won't commit to the spread?**

Our guides will adjust — reconfigure the spread, change the calling approach, or relocate the setup if birds are consistently flaring. Geese that are pressured or have seen heavy decoy traffic in an area will sometimes refuse. Part of what 20+ years of experience buys you is the ability to read the problem and make the right call quickly.
