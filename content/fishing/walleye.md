## Walleye Seasons on Lake Huron

### Spring: The Near-Shore Bite

Spring is when walleye fishing on Lake Huron's western shoreline comes alive. As water temperatures climb out of the low 40s and into the upper 40s and 50s, walleye push toward shallower near-shore structure. This is prime time for jigging — working the rocky points, gravel transitions, and nearshore flats that hold post-spawn fish looking to feed aggressively.

Jigging with soft plastics, blade baits, and live-bait rigs produces excellent results in the spring window, typically from late April through early June depending on the year. These fish are hungry and less pressured than they will be by midsummer. It's also the most visual, hands-on style of walleye fishing we run — anglers feel every tap.

### Summer: Trolling the Open Water

By June, walleye on Lake Huron pull off the shore and suspend in the cooler, deeper open water. This is when trolling becomes the primary and most productive presentation. Surface temperatures push into the 60s and 70s, and walleye drop down into the thermocline — often 20 to 40 feet deep or deeper depending on the week.

We run multi-rod trolling spreads targeting suspended walleye with crawler harnesses, deep-diving crankbaits, and body baits. Planer boards let us cover a wide swath of water, putting presentations at multiple depths simultaneously. This is a numbers game paired with precision — dialing in the right speed, the right depth, and the right color combination for the conditions that day.

Summer trolling on Lake Huron can be lights-out when you're on fish. Multiple-fish mornings are common. Big fish are always in the mix.

### Fall: Transition and Feeding Windows

September and October bring cooling water temperatures and a renewed aggressive bite as walleye stack up before winter. Fall trolling and jigging both produce well, and fish at this time of year tend to be heavy — fat from a summer of feeding. If you want your best shot at a 7 or 8-pound walleye, fall is the time to be on the water.

## Michigan Fishing License Requirements

Every angler age 17 and older is required to have a valid Michigan fishing license to fish on Lake Huron. Licenses are available online at michigan.gov/dnr or at most bait and sporting goods shops throughout Huron County.

For walleye specifically, no additional species stamp is required in Michigan beyond the base fishing license — but always check the current year's regulations, as rules are updated annually. Non-resident licenses are available for out-of-state guests.

We recommend purchasing your license before arriving so you're not scrambling the morning of your charter.
